About

I'm a recent graduate of Duke University, a Rhodes Scholar-elect, and an admitted student at Stanford Medical School. While at Duke, I studied healthcare delivery within the criminal justice system, led transfer student orientation, and conducted data science and chemical education research.

Over the course of transfer, medical school, and nationally competitive scholarship applications, I've learned how to construct a narrative, communicate it effectively, and prepare for high-stakes interviews. I enjoy teaching and sharing what I've learned, and I take on a small number of students through referral-based private mentoring.

Services

How I Work With Students

Admissions Coaching

I focus on application strategy — developing your narrative and communicating it effectively through essays and activity descriptions. For programs that require interviews, like medical school, I offer extensive preparation through original question design and rigorous mock interview practice with detailed feedback.

Particular expertise in undergraduate, transfer, medical school, and scholarship applications.

College Success Coaching

Many students feel overwhelmed by the transition to college or by the transitions within it: sophomore to junior year, beginning to recruit for jobs, finding community. As a transfer student, I experienced this myself. Success coaching means having someone who checks in with you, points you in the right direction, and helps you stay on track with deadlines, next steps, and personal goals.

Some clients transition from admissions coaching to success coaching as they move from one stage of education to the next.

Philosophy

"My goal is to guide students towards becoming the best they can be — not to be defined by admission committees, but to grow in their understanding of themselves."

I'm the oldest of four siblings, and my youngest brother has a learning disability. I am sensitive to how admissions can feel like a statement on one's worth. I want the best oucomes for students, but I also want the process to feel like it is inherently worth something.

I hope reflection for college applications can students to help them see themselves clearly, tell their story with confidence, and approach what comes next with intention.

The ability to your story has value far beyond admission to a specefic program.
